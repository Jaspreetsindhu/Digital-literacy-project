# Cyber Safety Checklist for Students

## General Cyber Safety

- [ ] Always verify links, emails, and messages are legitimate before clicking on them.
- [ ] Never share OTPs, passwords, or bank details with anyone — even those claiming to be from a trusted organization.
- [ ] Use a different password for each account and update them regularly.
- [ ] Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
- [ ] Avoid using public Wi-Fi for online banking or any sensitive activities.
- [ ] Keep all devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.
- [ ] Be cautious of messages that create urgency or fear (e.g., threats of account suspension).
- [ ] Regularly review your bank statements and transaction history for any suspicious activity.

## UPI & Financial Safety

- [ ] Never approve UPI payment requests or "collect requests" you did not initiate.
- [ ] Always double-check the receiver's name and UPI ID before sending money.
- [ ] Do not scan QR codes received from unknown sources or unsolicited senders.

## Reporting Cybercrime

- [ ] Report incidents at the **National Cyber Crime Portal**: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- [ ] Call the **Cyber Crime Helpline**: `1930` *(available 24×7 in India)*
