# Case Study:

Phishing is something that happens a lot on the internet. It is when bad people try to trick you into giving them information like your password or bank details. They do this by pretending to be someone you trust.
A phishing attack usually starts with an email or message that looks like it is from your bank or a website you use. The message will say something like "Your account will be blocked" or "Someone is using your account without permission". It will also have a link that you can click on to fix the problem.
If you click on the link you will go to a website that looks like the one.. It is not real. The people who made the website will ask you to enter your login details or bank information. If you do this they will get your information. Use it to do bad things like take your money or steal your identity.
Bad people often target students and young people because they might not know about these scams. If you fall for a phishing scam you could lose money. Have your personal information stolen.
This is a reminder to be careful when you get messages or emails, from people you do not know. You should always check to make sure they are real before you give them any information. Phishing is a problem and we need to be aware of it to stay safe online. Phishing attacks can happen to anyone so we all need to know about phishing and how to avoid it.
