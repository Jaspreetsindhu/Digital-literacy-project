Digital Literacy Project Report - CSE0001

Digital Literacy Introduction (100-150 words)

In today's fast changing digital world, being digitally literate is important for student success academically and professionally. I undertook this project as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy course where I acted as a Student Digital Ambassador. This project consists of creating an electronic portfolio that illustrates a student's knowledge, practical skills and appropriate use of digital technologies.

There are five objectives of the project, each corresponding to an area of digital literacy. These include increasing awareness of digital literacy, maintaining a professional online presence, using digital technology platforms, communicating respectfully and safely using technology and protecting oneself from cybercrime. Through this project, I have gained practical experience with many digital tools and understand more about responsible and safe digital behaviour.


Task 1


Digital literacy is vital for today’s students, as they spend increasing amounts of time using technology. Creating and sharing digital literacy content can be daunting, but it also builds confidence and prepares students to be responsible digital citizens.

To help raise awareness of digital literacy, I created a digital literacy infographic using Canva, a very simple online design application. The infographic serves as a complete visual resource intended to aid student understanding of the importance of digital literacy. The infographic contains three major sections covering safe use of the internet, helpful digital resources for students, and the importance of professional online profiles.

To design the infographic, I used many icons, bullet points, and other visually attractive graphics to assist with understanding and interest. In addition, I attempted to create a simple design while portraying information clearly and informatively.

My main obstacle while creating the infographic was balancing the amount of information provided with the visual attractiveness of the document. I found this to be a challenge, but it succeeded in expanding my knowledge and skill in creating effective visual representations of information. Furthermore, it was interestingly enjoyable because it involved both creative planning and technical implementation.


🌐 Task Two: Professional Portfolio (150–200 words)

Created/updating my professional profiles on platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn was another task I completed in this module. For students, these types of platforms are important in that they provide effective ways to demonstrate skills, projects, and educational accomplishments.

For the GitHub profile, I made a README that included my name, branch, year, and a short statement regarding my learning aspirations. I used this as a digital introduction for anyone who visits my GitHub profile. I updated my LinkedIn educational profile by including my current enrolment details and anticipated graduation date.

There are many distinct purposes of both GitHub and LinkedIn, but for the student, GitHub is primarily a place to demonstrate coding projects and skills, while LinkedIn is primarily an avenue for networking and professional visibility.

Over the next four years, I intend on uploading my projects to GitHub so that I can increase my coding portfolio. Furthermore, I will use LinkedIn to develop connections with professionals, seek information about internships, and remain current on industry news. Finally, completing this task helped me realize the value of establishing a professional digital persona early on.
